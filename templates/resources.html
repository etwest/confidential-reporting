Resources

This is an example of providing resources to users of the website. Pulled from the University of California, Santa Cruz.

CARE - Campus Advocacy Resources & Education

For: survivors of sexual violence, dating violence, and stalking (students, staff and faculty)

What they do: Advocate and support survivors of sexual violence

How they do it:

     -inform survivors of their rights and options (legal, medical, and Title IX)

     -create physical safety plans

     -provides emotional support and counseling

     -academic, housing, employment accommodations

     -accompaniment to Title IX and/or other criminal proceedings

     -prevention education for campus community

Privacy: Confidential

Location:Oakes Academic Building R-221, above westside writing center

Contact Information: care@ucsc.edu, 831-502-2273


SHOP - Student Health Outreach & Promotion

For: all students

What they do: Support students around alcohol/drug use, sexual health, and pregnancy

How they do it:

     -answer questions about sexual health, birth control, and pregnancy

     -free and anonymous HIV testing via cheek swab

     -affordable safer sex supplies

     -sexual/reproductive health workshops

     -education about safe partying

Privacy: Anonymous

Location: Student Health Center, to the right of the pharmacy

Contact Information: shop@ucsc.edu, Sexual Health: 831-459-2625, Alcohol/Drug Support: 831-459-1417


CAPS - Counseling and Psychological Services

For: all students

What they do: Provide mental health services, including therapy and psychiatry

How they do it:

     -short term individual therapy

     -psychiatry

     -referrals to long-term therapy off campus

     -in person Crisis Counseling, Monday - Friday 8 AM to 5 PM

     -drop in, informal, and information sessions for support, advice, and information

     -workshops on stress management, mindfulness, and self-compassion

Privacy: Confidential

Location: Student Health Center, 2nd floor, above the pharmacy

Contact Information: caps@ucsc.edu, 24 Hour Crisis Hotline: 831-459-2628


Title IX

For: students, staff, and faculty

What they do: Respond to, investigate, and resolve reports of sexual violence, sexual-harassment, and gender-based violence

How they do it:

     -neutrally address complaints of sexual violence through investigations and alternative resolutions

     -provides adjustments for housing, academic, employment, and safety concerns

     -gives their findings to Student Conduct, which then decides on disciplinary action

Privacy: Information shared on a need-to-know basis

Location: Kerr Hall 106

Contact Information: titleix@ucsc.edu, 831-459-2462

Education Opportunity Program

For:

     -students who are being educationally disadvantaged which includes attending an under-resourced high school with limited opportunities and/or attending a community college with low-transfer rates.
     -students who participated in pre-university Educational Preparation Programs (e.g. Early Academic Outreach, Upward Bond, MESA, Talent Search, Puente, etc.).

Location: Academic Resource Center
